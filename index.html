"Before we begin this grand spiritual adventure, perhaps we should test these methods scientifically. Let me conduct a small experiment."

Intrigued, Akbar agreed. The next day, Birbal announced a contest in the royal court. "His Majesty wishes to reward the person who can have the most extraordinary spiritual experience using any method they choose. The winner will receive a thousand gold coins!"

5

Word spread throughout the kingdom, and soon the palace was filled with spiritual seekers, each demonstrating their technique. One man sat in perfect stillness, claiming he could remain motionless for days. Another chanted "Ram Ram Ram" so continuously that he spoke of nothing else. A third spun in circles until he collapsed, insisting he had touched the divine.

After watching for hours, Akbar turned to Birbal in confusion. "These people all claim to be having profound experiences, but they look rather... ordinary."

6

Just then, an old beggar named Gopal shuffled into the court. His clothes were torn, his hair wild, and he carried nothing but a simple bowl. "Your Majesty," he wheezed, "I heard about your contest. May I participate?"

The courtiers laughed. This ragged man clearly knew nothing about sophisticated spiritual practices. "What is your method?" Akbar asked kindly.

"What do you mean?"

Gopal looked puzzled. "Right now I'm talking with you. Earlier I was walking. Before that, I was sitting in the shade listening to birds. I don't understand what you mean by 'experiences.' This is just... life happening."

"But don't you want something more?" Akbar asked. "Some greater meaning? Some ultimate satisfaction?"

"Who would want it?" Gopal replied simply. "When I'm thirsty, water satisfies. When I'm cold, warmth satisfies. When I'm lonely, conversation satisfies. What else is there to want?"

The next morning, Akbar announced his decision about the contest: "No one."

He explained, "Every contestant was trying to achieve, collect, or experience something special. But what they were seeking was already present in the very awareness that was seeking it."

From that day forward, Emperor Akbar ruled his kingdom with a different quality of attention. Not because he had found something new, but because he had stopped looking for something other than what was already fully present in each moment of imperial life.

And whenever anyone asked Birbal about the Emperor's great spiritual transformation, he would simply smile and say: "His Majesty discovered that the king he was trying to become was already sitting on the throne."
